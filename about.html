Rooted in Christ, Growing in Faith

Welcome to ACK St. John's Happy Valley Parish Thika. We are a vibrant Anglican community dedicated to worship, service, and spiritual growth. Join us as we journey together in faith and love.

Introduction

The ACK St. John Happy Valley Parish identity is defined through its Vision, Mission and Core Values. The three amplify the focus to serve God and His people and they will be the ‘Light of the Parish’ for the next five (5) years.

Vision

To be a vibrant Anglican Church that is Christ-centered, Spirit-filled and committed to transforming lives.A Christ-Centred Parish for holistic growth.

Mission

Proclaim the Gospel to achieve God’s purpose.

Core Values

  • Integrity:We uphold the tenets of honesty and truth while serving our congregants, partners, internal and external stakeholders and promote these values as enshrined in the Gospel.
  • Commitment:We serve all our members and the community equally and impartially with total devotion and strive to express care and love to the disadvantaged in the society.
  • Stewardship:We keep custody of the resources entrusted to us and prudently manage them for the Parish to achieve the desired results.
  • Evangelism:Evangelism is the cornerstone of the Parish and we are dedicated to serve our members and reach out to the community with the teachings of Jesus Christ our saviour.
  • Cohesion:We foster unity amongst our members as a means to achieve holistic growth of the Parish.

OUR HISTORY

History of the Parish The ACK St. John’s Happy Valley Parish, (here in after referred to as The Parish), is located in Happy Valley Estate, Kamenu Location, Thika West Sub-County in Kiambu County. It is about 10km from Thika town off Thika-Garissa road. The Parish started in February 1997 as a small church in Makongeni Parish with a membership of four (4) ladies in a rented open plot. The congregation continued to grow and in August 1997 the members made a decision to search for a plot and build a Church. A Building Committee was formed to undertake the task. A funds drive was conducted in August 1998 where Two Hundred and Fifty Thousand Kenya Shillings (KShs. 250,000) was realised and a plot was purchased. The congregants continued with their determination by conducting periodical fund raisers and construction of the Church (currently the Church Hall) commenced in May 2000 and was completed in March 2001 and remained under Makongeni Parish. The dedication stone was laid on 6th April 2001 and unveiled on 13th August 2006 by Rt. Rev. Dr. Gideon G. Githiga, the then Bishop of ACK Thika Diocese. The Church continued to attract more worshippers and by May 2001 it was almost full. The growth in membership prompted the elders to focus on the future in view of the rapid increase in population in the estate. A decision was, therefore, made to search for a bigger plot to build a Church that could match the increase in number of congregants and future growth of the estate. In 2001, the Building Committee’s mandate was expanded to include all aspects of Church development and renamed the Development Committee. The chairman of the committee was mandated to search for a plot and by the Grace of God a bigger one adjacent to the Church was available at a negotiated price of Kshs 520,000. A fund raiser conducted in November 2003 raised Kshs 450,000 for the purchase of the plot. The remaining amount was borrowed from the members and the plot was bought and transferred to the Church. In 2006, the chairman of the Development Committee and some of the members visited ACK Kahawa Sukari Church for benchmarking. After the visit, the team embarked on designing a modern Church, planning and fund raising for its construction. Periodical fund raisers were conducted for the construction works as follows:

Governance Structure

A formal structure exists to ensure successful operations and management of the Parish. In the Anglican setting, the Bishop is the head of the Diocese and the Vicar heads the Parish. The Parish’s operations have been structured into departments and committees that work closely with each other as many parts but one body of the Church; to ensure smooth running of all Parish activities. The Parish Church Council (PCC) provides the overall management of Parish programmes and affairs ranging from spiritual to administrative duties. Currently, six (6) departments have been very instrumental to the spiritual growth and infrastructure development of the Parish. These are:

Departments

  • Kenya Anglican Men’s Association (KAMA)
  • Mothers’ Union (MU)
  • Kenya Anglican Youth Organisation (KAYO)
  • Children’s Ministry – Sunday School and Brigade
  • Choir
  • Praise and Worship

In order to ensure sustainability and accountability, the Parish has various committees charged with different responsibilities. The committees include:

  • Executive Committee
  • Finance Committee
  • Development Committee
In addition, the PCC forms ad-hoc committees on need basis. Following an initiative of PCC and the Development Committee, the congregants were divided into four clusters that serve a duo purpose; prayer cells and resource mobilisation. These are:
  • 1. Central
  • 2. Plains View
  • 3. Mountain View
  • 4. Riverside
In addition, there are two very vibrant but independent associations, the Men’s Welfare and Ladies Welfare. Although their focus is the social welfare of their members, their participation in the development of the Parish has been extremely encouraging. The Men’s Welfare Association, for example, in 2005 donated funds that were used in the construction of two Sunday school classrooms next to the Church Hall.
